Translations (I18n)

The objective of this document and its sub pages is to give an overview of how translations for the Maven app work and how you, as an engineer or otherwise, can be part of this on-going effort.

Introduction

As AMI's list of partners grows and our user base goes global, we must internationalize and localize our app so that more people can use it in their preferred language.

Internationalization is the process of making all strings in our app "translatable", and localization is the process of providing translations and localized formats to these "translatable" strings. To make a string "translatable" means that it we'll no longer have such string directly in our codebase, but instead will have a reference to it. For example, instead of having Welcome to AMI hardcoded in one of our Ruby files, we will have a reference: mt('welcome'). That way, mt('welcome') can dynamically render an appropriate string on the user language of choice when visting our welcome page. In other words, Bienvenido a AMI will render if the user's language of choice is Spanish, or Welcome to AMI if their language of choice is English (or if they don't have a choice, as English is a fallback).

But then, where do the strings Bienvenido a AMI and Welcome to AMI live if we only have references (a.k.a. keys) in our codebase? The answer is in Lokalise.

Lokalise is a 3rd party service that holds the mappings between the references/keys (like welcome in the example above) and the actual translations for all supported languges (like Bienvenido a AMI and Welcome to AMI from the example). By having everything in Lokalise, strings will be automatically translated by machines, or they can be edited manually by us or professional translators, at any time. Our codebase no longer blocks or slows down the process of creating translation in new languages or updating exiting ones. And the AMI app will automagically pull translated strings from Lokalise, thus keeping us up-to-date and always localized!
